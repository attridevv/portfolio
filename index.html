poetry

Life Happened
Blue Eyes
Gray Dreams

Life happened, me waiting. Believing it would stop, it would wait. Life happening and happening, consistent like spring water.

Oh water, don't reflect what I see. Once five, now twenty—what changed? The vessel grew wings, then they were cut, and strings were pulled.

Cut these strings before they strangle me. Was the wind too strong, or were the strings too tight? Either way, the reflection doesn't lie.

Oh water, let's start again. Take me away to the far sea, where I can sleep once again. — Dev

What beautiful colour of your eyes, that a boy loses his perception of time

I have seen the endless seas but I see depth in your eyes farseen. Blessed were my eyes to have met yours.

Flower meant a thing to me, now it means things to me. Is it Rose that you like, or cherry blossom alike?

My heart and eyes are at war, but it seems to be a losing war. Delhi to Darwin seems to be my next flight.

Home is where the heart is, and my eyes are where your eyes is. — Dev

Oh shades of light, show me the way, through darkness that swallows and night that turns gray.

What I seek is not freedom, as seeking confines. And what I like is not love, but a flicker that shines.

White is not always warm, and black not always stark. In the gray of our dreams, all our truths can be seen. — Dev
