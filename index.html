What is the meaning of life?

+
my take

To be human — before anything else. To create something that outlasts you, to leave a legacy that speaks when you can't. But being human isn't a given — it's a practice. It's having emotions and expressing them. It's understanding and being understood. It's loving something enough to let it change you. To live is not the same as to be human — one can survive without ever truly living. The meaning, then, is this: don't just live. Be human. Create. Feel. Leave something behind.

When did we stop asking questions?

+
my take

Every kid asks a hundred questions before the world teaches them to stop. We traded curiosity for compliance — accepting statements without a counter-thought. The environment shaped us, and I don't blame anyone for that. But I refuse to stay there. The point isn't who took our questions — it's whether we're willing to take them back. To ask again, to seek understanding with curiosity instead of accepting answers with a closed mind.

Why does being drunk make us happy?

+
my take

Because it strips away what we've been conditioned to carry. No judgment. No outcomes. No weight of "what if." Just the moment — felt with true, unguarded emotion. Being drunk lifts you to a version of yourself that already exists underneath all the conditioning. But it's borrowed freedom. The real question is: what if we could live like that without the alcohol? What if we could be that free, that honest, that alive — on our own?
